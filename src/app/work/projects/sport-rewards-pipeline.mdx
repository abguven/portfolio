## TL;DR

- **The Problem:** A startup wanted to financially reward employees for cycling to work or doing sports, but relying on self-declarations created a massive fraud and overpayment risk.
- **The Solution:** An automated streaming pipeline that ingests Strava activities, cross-references them with Google Maps GPS data, and calculates strict eligibility through a Medallion Architecture.
- **The Impact:** Exposed a **40% gap** between declared and verified activities, saving the business **€69,670** in simulated overpayments.

## The Architecture

![Architecture Overview](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/abguven/sport-rewards-pipeline/main/assets/pipeline-overview.png)

## Engineering Highlights

**Zero-Impact CDC Streaming** — Used Debezium to read PostgreSQL's WAL via logical replication. Data is sent to Redpanda instantly without polling or slowing down the source database.

**Chaos Engineering & Data Quality** — Injected a Chaos Monkey (future dates, impossible speeds) into the simulator. Great Expectations gates the Bronze layer, isolating bad data without crashing the streaming job.

**Decoupled Business Logic** — All rules (bonus rates, distance limits) are stored in an external JSON file. The Finance team can update a threshold and Spark applies it instantly — no code change or Docker rebuild required.

**Smart AI Notifications** — An Airflow batch triggers Mistral AI bi-weekly to generate a pool of natural-language Slack messages, preventing alert fatigue.

## Results

By pushing the Gold-layer data to Power BI, HR and Finance were provided with a real-time audit tool rather than a spreadsheet.

> **Financial Snapshot — 12-month simulation, 161 employees**
> - Declarative payout: €172,480
> - GPS-verified payout: €102,810
> - **Fraud / Error Risk detected: €69,670**
