## Overview

Simulated business case — **"Academy"** is a fictional EdTech startup offering online courses for high school and university students. Facing a strategic expansion decision, the data team received the following mandate:

> *"Which countries have the highest potential student base for our platform — and how will that potential evolve?"*

The raw material: the **World Bank EdStats dataset** — 5 CSV files, **886,930 rows** in the main file alone, and **3,665 indicators** covering every measurable dimension of global education from 1970 to 2100. No pre-selection, no pre-defined targets.

## The 3-Stage Indicator Pipeline

The core challenge wasn't the analysis itself — it was **taming the scope**. A 3-stage filtering pipeline reduced 3,665 indicators to 6 statistically independent, business-relevant signals.

![Indicator selection pipeline — 3,665 → 3,120 → 15 → 6](/images/projects/p2/pipeline_funnel.png)

### Stage 1 — Business Filter

Retained only indicator categories directly relevant to the EdTech business question:
- **Infrastructure / Communications** — Can users actually connect to the platform?
- **Health / Population Dynamics** — Is the target youth market growing?
- **Education Expenditures** — Do societies already invest in education?

### Stage 2 — Data Quality Filter

The dataset contains projections up to **year 2100**, stored in the exact same format as real observed data. Without profiling, these hypothetical values would have silently polluted the analysis.

**Decision:** Excluded all future projections (2020–2100). The **2013–2017 window** delivered the best balance between recency and data completeness.

![Fill rate by year — historical data peaks at 2013–2017](/images/projects/p2/fill_rate_past_years.png)

![Future projections fill rate — uniformly ~7% across 2020–2100](/images/projects/p2/fill_rate_future_years.png)

![Top 15 indicators by fill rate](/images/projects/p2/indicator_fill_rate.png)

### Stage 3 — Statistical Filter

Applied **Spearman correlation** to detect redundancy. Any pair with |r| > 0.70 was flagged; the less business-relevant indicator was dropped.

![Spearman correlation before deduplication — 15×15 matrix](/images/projects/p2/correlation_before.png)

![Spearman correlation after deduplication — 6×6 matrix, near-zero off-diagonal](/images/projects/p2/correlation_after.png)

## Scoring Model & Results

Each country received a **weighted potential score** via a normalized weighted average of 5 retained indicators (MinMaxScaler from Scikit-learn).

| Indicator | Meaning | Weight |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| `IT.NET.USER.P2` | Internet users per 100 people | **35%** |
| `SP.POP.TOTL` | Total population | **25%** |
| `SP.POP.1564.TO.ZS` | Population ages 15–64 (%) | **15%** |
| `SP.POP.GROW` | Annual population growth (%) | **15%** |
| `SE.COM.DURS` | Duration of compulsory education | **10%** |

> `SL.UEM.TOTL.ZS` (unemployment) was deliberately excluded: it can indicate both instability and demand for retraining. Including it would have introduced noise rather than signal.

![Top 5 countries by EdTech market potential score](/images/projects/p2/top5_countries.png)

### Two Strategic Market Profiles

| Profile | Countries | Key driver |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| **Volume market** | China | Colossal population — even a tiny market share represents massive absolute revenue |
| **Quality markets** | Luxembourg, UK, Iceland, Norway | High internet penetration + strong education culture — easier to convert, higher LTV |

## Technical Challenges

**Future projections hidden inside historical data** — The dataset stores year-2100 projections with no flag differentiating them from real observations. Profiling fill rates by year revealed a structural break at 2018 where completeness collapses from ~30% to ~7%.

**3,665 indicators to a non-redundant shortlist** — Manual review is not feasible. Built a systematic 3-stage pipeline — business relevance → data completeness → statistical independence. Every cut is documented and reproducible.

**"Fake countries" contaminating the dataset** — `EdStatsCountry` contains regional and income-group aggregates ("World", "Sub-Saharan Africa") alongside sovereign nations. Filtered using the `Region` column (aggregates have no region assigned).

**Transparent, defensible scoring model** — Every weight is explicitly justified. The model is fully parameterized — changing a weight is a one-line edit, making it trivial to re-run for a Top 10 or with alternative strategic assumptions.

**Missing values without imputation** — Used temporal aggregation (mean per country-indicator pair across 2013–2017) rather than imputation. Countries with fewer than 2 valid years were flagged but retained.
