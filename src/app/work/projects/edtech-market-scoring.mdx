## TL;DR

- **The Problem:** An EdTech startup needed to identify the top international markets for expansion — out of 195 countries and 3,665 indicators covering every measurable dimension of global education from 1970 to 2100.
- **The Solution:** A 3-stage indicator selection pipeline (business filter → data quality filter → statistical filter) that reduced 3,665 indicators to 6 independent, business-relevant signals, then scored every country on a weighted composite index.
- **The Impact:** 5 actionable market recommendations across two strategic profiles — one volume market and four quality markets.

## The 3-Stage Pipeline

![Indicator selection funnel — 3,665 → 15 → 6](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/abguven/edtech-market-expansion-eda/main/assets/pipeline_funnel.png)

**Stage 1 — Business Filter** — Retained only indicators directly relevant to the EdTech question: internet infrastructure (can users connect?), population dynamics (is the youth market growing?), and education expenditure (does society already invest in education?).

**Stage 2 — Data Quality Filter** — The dataset stores year-2100 projections in the exact same format as real observed data — with no flag differentiating them. Profiling fill rates by year revealed a structural break at 2018 where completeness collapses from ~30% to ~7%. Everything from 2020 onwards was excluded. The **2013–2017 window** delivered the best balance between recency and completeness.

**Stage 3 — Statistical Filter** — Spearman correlation detected redundancy. Any pair with |r| > 0.70 was flagged; the less business-relevant indicator was dropped. Result: 6 statistically independent signals.

![Correlation after deduplication — 6×6, near-zero off-diagonal](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/abguven/edtech-market-expansion-eda/main/assets/correlation_after.png)

## Scoring Model

Each country scored via a normalized weighted average (MinMaxScaler), with every weight explicitly justified:

| Indicator | Meaning | Weight |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| `IT.NET.USER.P2` | Internet users per 100 people | **35%** — eliminatory criterion |
| `SP.POP.TOTL` | Total population | **25%** — total addressable market |
| `SP.POP.1564.TO.ZS` | Population ages 15–64 (%) | **15%** — future student pool |
| `SP.POP.GROW` | Annual population growth (%) | **15%** — market dynamism |
| `SE.COM.DURS` | Duration of compulsory education | **10%** — education culture proxy |

> `SL.UEM.TOTL.ZS` (unemployment) deliberately excluded — it can signal both instability and demand for retraining. Including it would have introduced noise rather than signal.

![Top 5 countries by market potential score](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/abguven/edtech-market-expansion-eda/main/assets/top5_countries.png)

## Results — Two Strategic Profiles

| Profile | Countries | Key driver |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| **Volume market** | China | Colossal population — even a tiny share = massive revenue |
| **Quality markets** | Luxembourg, UK, Iceland, Norway | High internet penetration + strong education culture |

## Engineering Highlights

**"Fake countries" contaminating the dataset** — `EdStatsCountry` contains regional aggregates ("World", "Sub-Saharan Africa", "Low income") alongside sovereign nations. Including them would have inflated population metrics. Filtered via the `Region` column — aggregates have no region assigned.

**Transparent, defensible scoring model** — Every weight is documented with a business justification. The model is fully parameterized — changing a weight is a one-line edit, making it trivial to re-run for a Top 10 or with alternative strategic assumptions. No black boxes.

**Missing values without imputation** — Temporal aggregation (mean per country-indicator across 2013–2017) rather than imputation. Countries with fewer than 2 valid years were flagged but retained — keeping the dataset honest rather than artificially complete.
